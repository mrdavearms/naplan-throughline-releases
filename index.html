Opening it the first time — one quick step

These are free builds that aren't paid code-signed, so your computer shows a one-time warning. It's expected and safe — here's how to get past it (you only do this once):

On a Mac

  1. Open the downloaded .dmg and drag NAPLAN Cohort Tracker into your Applications folder.
  2. In Applications, right-click (or Control-click) the app → Open → Open.
  3. Still blocked? Open System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down, and click Open Anyway.

On Windows

  1. Run the downloaded setup .exe.
  2. If you see a blue “Windows protected your PC” box, click More info → Run anyway.

Early release  May not work properly

This is an early release and is still being tested, so some figures or screens may not work as expected — please check anything important against your source spreadsheets before you rely on it. Year 3 → Year 5 (primary) cohort tracking is the newest part and the least validated, so treat primary figures as especially provisional.

Your feedback is genuinely welcome — tell me what works, what doesn't, and anything that looks off: dave.armstrong@education.vic.gov.au.

What it does

Point it at a folder of your NAPLAN SSSR files and it shows participation, proficiency, equity and skill gaps — and tracks the same students across two years (Year 3 to Year 5, or Year 7 to Year 9), the closest thing to a true measure of your school's contribution. It builds tidy PDF reports for your leadership and faculty conversations.

Private by design  Everything runs on your computer. No student names appear anywhere, and no data is ever uploaded.
